Pool Building Services in McAllen, TX

Three ways we build and rebuild backyard pools across the Rio Grande Valley — gunite construction, fiberglass installation, and full pool remodeling.

Gunite Pool Construction

Gunite pools are built shape by shape on your property — steel rebar first, then a sprayed concrete shell, curing, interior finish, and decking. Because nothing is pre-formed, you get full control over size, depth, and layout, including tanning ledges, spas, and custom steps.

Homeowners choose gunite when they want a pool that matches an unusual lot shape, or when they're planning water features and decking that need to be built as one connected project. It costs more and takes longer than fiberglass, but the shell can last 50 years with periodic resurfacing.

Fiberglass Pool Installation

Fiberglass pools arrive as a single pre-formed shell that gets set into an excavated hole, leveled, plumbed, and backfilled. Because there's no curing time for concrete, most fiberglass installs are finished and swimmable within one to two weeks of the first shovel.

The surface is smooth and non-porous, so algae has a harder time taking hold and you'll spend less on chemicals over time. It's a good fit if you want a straightforward install without the multi-month timeline of a gunite build.

Pool Remodeling & Resurfacing

If your existing pool is cracking, leaking, or just looks dated, a remodel can bring it back without a full teardown. That includes resurfacing the interior finish, retiling the waterline, updating decking, and adding features like a sun shelf, spa, or water feature to a pool that was built years ago.

Ignoring a cracked shell or failing plaster usually makes the repair more expensive later, since water finds its way into small cracks and widens them over time. Catching it early keeps the fix smaller.
